What is this about iDescribe the real-world situation you're simulating in plain language. Example: "A group of young adults is exposed to increasingly persuasive social media content over 12 weeks. We want to understand who gets drawn in, who resists, and whether the group splits or stays together." Without this, the simulated people interact in a void — their conversations are generic and meaningless. missing
Who are the people iDescribe the group being simulated. What makes them different from each other? Example: "25% are highly engaged and struggle to step back, 50% have normal usage, 25% are disciplined and set clear limits. Younger participants (16-20) tend to be more easily drawn in." Without this, the personality types are just random — not grounded in any real population. missing
What would surprise you iNot "what should happen" but "what would make you doubt the simulation." Think in two layers:

Structural (checked automatically): Does the group split into distinct behavioral patterns? Do individual outliers exist? Do interventions actually change anything? Is the system predictably unstable or just random noise?

Surprise bounds (you define): "I'd be surprised if nobody at all crossed into problematic territory" or "I'd be surprised if the entire group ended up in the same place." These aren't targets — they're sanity checks. The sim is free to produce unexpected results, but flags anything outside what you'd consider remotely plausible. missing
What help exists iWhen things go wrong in the real world, what can be done? Example: "If someone shows signs of compulsive use for 3 weeks straight, offer a reflection prompt. If someone approaches a critical threshold, flag it. No more than 2 interventions per observation cycle — too many backfire." Without this, the simulation uses generic intervention types that may not match how your domain actually works. optional
How time passes iWhat does one step in the simulation represent in real time? Are there phases or turning points? Example: "Each step is one week. Weeks 1-4 are a baseline period. Weeks 5-8 introduce the main challenge. Weeks 9-12 measure whether people recovered or got stuck." Without this, time is just abstract steps — the simulation can't distinguish an early-phase reaction from a late-stage pattern. optional
